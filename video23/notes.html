  1. Inline elements behave like text inside a line.
  2. Inline: width and height do not work.
  3. Inline: margin-left and margin-right work.
  4. Inline: margin-top and margin-bottom do not work.
  5. Inline: padding-left and padding-right work.
  6. Inline: padding-top and padding-bottom apply visually but do not push other elements vertically.
  7. Block elements start on a new line and (by default) take the full available width.
  8. Block: all margins and paddings (top, bottom, left, right) behave normally.
  9. Inline-block sits inline (can appear beside other boxes) but supports the block box model.
  10. Inline-block: you can place multiple inline-block boxes on the same line (if space allows) and each supports width, height, and all margins & paddings.

Inline-block lets you place multiple block-like boxes on the same line. Each box supports width, height, margin (top/right/bottom/left) and padding (top/right/bottom/left), but they flow inline and wrap to the next line when space runs out.
